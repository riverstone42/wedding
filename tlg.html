Carpool Lanes vs. Express Lanes

Southern California freeways can be confusing because carpool (HOV) lanes and toll express lanes sometimes look very similar and may even share the same lane corridor.

Please Read · Do Not Skip

It is very easy to drift onto a toll road by accident — please use caution.

Toll entrances can appear suddenly, and signage often gives you only a few seconds to react. If you are not paying close attention, you may end up on an Express Lane or toll road before you realize it.

If you do accidentally enter a toll road or Express Lane, you are still responsible for payment. An honest mistake does not waive the toll, and unpaid tolls can turn into violation notices and added fees.

How to pay for accidental toll travel — the procedure differs by freeway:

  • I-10 & I-110 (LA Metro Express Lanes) Wait for a "Notice of Toll Evasion" to arrive in the mail. The agency photographs your license plate and mails a bill to the vehicle's registered owner covering the posted toll plus a processing fee (currently $9), due within 30 days to avoid added penalties. [1, 2]
  • SR-73, 133, 241, 261 (The Toll Roads — Orange County) Do not wait for a notice. Pay online promptly after your trip (typically within 5 days) using the "One-Time Toll" option at thetollroads.com, or set up an account, to avoid penalties.
  • SR-91 Express Lanes (Riverside/Orange County) A transponder is required to use these lanes. If you entered without one, contact the 91 Express Lanes customer service promptly to arrange payment rather than waiting for a violation notice.

Here is how the two lane types differ:

  • Carpool (HOV) lanes are generally free for vehicles meeting the posted occupancy requirements (typically 2 or more people, but requirements vary by freeway).
  • Express Lanes are toll lanes that charge a fee based on traffic conditions.
  • On some freeways, carpools may travel free or at a reduced toll in the Express Lanes, but the rules vary by location.
  • If you enter an Express Lane without the proper toll account or transponder, you may receive a toll charge or violation notice.

Important: Before using a lane marked "Express Lanes," check the signs carefully. If you are unfamiliar with Southern California tolling systems, the easiest option is to use your navigation app's "Avoid Tolls" setting.

Navigation Apps

Google Maps, Apple Maps, and Waze generally identify toll roads and Express Lanes. If you prefer not to deal with tolls, enable "Avoid Tolls" before starting your trip.

Wedding Guest Recommendation

For most wedding guests visiting Southern California for a few days, we recommend either:

  1. Using a rental car and selecting the rental company's toll program if you expect to drive extensively, or
  2. Turning on "Avoid Tolls" in your navigation app and using the regular freeway lanes.

This avoids most toll-related issues while still allowing convenient travel throughout the Los Angeles and Orange County areas.
